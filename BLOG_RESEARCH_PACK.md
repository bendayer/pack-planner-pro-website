## Post 2: Dog walking invoicing - how to invoice clients properly

### Useful UK-backed facts
- HMRC says self-employed people must keep records of all sales and income, all business expenses, and proof such as sales invoices, receipts, and bank statements.
- HMRC says records must be accurate and business transactions must be identifiable.
- HMRC says self-employed records generally need to be kept for at least 5 years after the 31 January submission deadline for the relevant tax year.
- If a business is VAT-registered, HMRC requires valid VAT invoices and copies of invoices issued.

### What this means for the blog
- The post can safely explain two levels:
  1. what a non-VAT dog walker should include as good practice
  2. what changes if the business is VAT-registered
- It should avoid implying that every sole trader must issue a formal VAT invoice if they are not VAT-registered.

### Practical blog structure
- Client name and address
- Your business name and contact details
- Invoice number
- Invoice date
- Date or period of walks/services
- Description of service
- Amount due
- Payment terms
- Payment method
- Late-payment wording if relevant

## Post 6: How to start a dog walking business in the UK in 2026

### Useful UK-backed facts
- GOV.UK says most small businesses start as sole traders or limited companies, and sole trader is the simplest structure to set up and keep records for.
- GOV.UK says you must register as a sole trader for Self Assessment if you earn more than £1,000 in a tax year from self-employment.
- GOV.UK says self-employed people must keep records of business income and expenses.
- GOV.UK says allowable self-employed expenses can be deducted when calculating taxable profit.

### Dog-walking-specific rules worth handling carefully
- There is not a single UK-wide "dog walker licence" requirement shown in the national animal activity licensing framework.
- GOV.UK and council guidance show licences are required for activities such as boarding, dog day care, breeding, selling pets, and exhibition/training in certain contexts.
- Council and GOV.UK guidance on Public Spaces Protection Orders confirms some councils limit the number of dogs that can be walked at once in certain public spaces, and this can apply to professional dog walkers.

### Safe, accurate way to frame this in the blog
- Register the business correctly
- keep records from day one
- check insurance before trading
- check local council rules and park restrictions
- do not assume the same dog numbers or access rules apply everywhere
